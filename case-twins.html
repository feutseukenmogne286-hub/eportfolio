Twins’ Service Encounter

Evaluating a hospital service experience to improve patient and family satisfaction.

Quick Facts

  • Course: Not provided in the report
  • Type: Individual case analysis
  • Deliverables: Written report using service marketing frameworks
  • Tools: Not provided in the report

Objective

Analyze a healthcare service encounter and recommend actions to improve patient and family satisfaction.

My Role

  • Classified health care along the tangible–intangible continuum.
  • Built a servuction model showing customers, providers and support systems.
  • Examined the hospital’s molecular model to identify service components.
  • Synthesized findings into a report and recommended improvements.

Approach & Frameworks

  • Used the “Scale of Market Entities” to position health care between investment management and consulting.
  • Constructed a servuction model to map visible and invisible service elements.
  • Referenced the hospital molecular model to assess people, process and physical evidence.
  • Compared different units (NICU vs. grower room) to identify gaps.

Key Insights

  • Health care remains an intangible‑dominant service despite equipment and supplies; empathy and communication matter most.
  • Frontline staff, including non‑medical personnel, influence patient stress levels.
  • Disjointed coordination between units leads to inconsistent care.
  • Room conditions and cleanliness affect overall satisfaction.

Recommendations & Output

  • Train frontline staff (security, orderlies) in empathy and emergency response.
  • Improve communication so parents understand who is caring for them and what to expect.
  • Coordinate across units to deliver consistent support from NICU to the grower room.
  • Upgrade room comfort and cleanliness.

Reflection

This project taught me how to break down service experiences into tangible frameworks. Small operational details, like a security guard’s tone or the cleanliness of a room, can shape perceptions of care. Next time, I’d collect patient feedback to validate and refine these insights.
