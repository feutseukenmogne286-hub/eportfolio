Balzac’s Nitro Cold Brew Relaunch

Developing a strategic plan to relaunch Balzac’s ready‑to‑drink coffee line.

Quick Facts

  • Course: Not provided in the report
  • Type: Group project
  • Deliverables: Comprehensive marketing plan and final report
  • Tools: Secondary research (Passport: Coffee in Canada)

Objective

Develop a strategic plan to relaunch Balzac’s Nitro Cold Brew, set measurable objectives for growth and sustainability, and outline a marketing mix plan.

My Role

  • Researched market size and growth trends for coffee and ready‑to‑drink beverages in Canada.
  • Analysed Balzac’s brand strengths, weaknesses and current product line.
  • Helped define the target market (young, on‑the‑go consumers seeking premium, sustainable beverages).
  • Co‑developed objectives and the marketing mix (product, packaging, distribution, promotion).
  • Assisted in drafting the timeline for product development and rollout.

Approach & Frameworks

  • Conducted secondary research using sources like Passport: Coffee in Canada and company reports.
  • Identified trends such as premiumization, sustainability and innovation driving coffee growth.
  • Evaluated competitor ready‑to‑drink brands and noted gaps in flavour variety and eco‑friendly packaging.
  • Proposed objectives: grow RTD revenue by 10 % by Q4 2026; increase sales volume by 25 % in key provinces; launch two new limited‑edition flavours by Summer 2026; reduce packaging waste by 20 %.
  • Outlined a quarterly rollout plan for 2026, including packaging design, flavour launches and distribution expansion.

Key Insights

  • The coffee market in Canada grew to CAD 2.9 billion in retail value sales in 2024 and is projected to reach CAD 3.7 billion by 2029.
  • Consumers value premium quality, sustainable sourcing and innovative flavours.
  • Balzac’s heritage and eco‑conscious practices provide a competitive advantage in the ready‑to‑drink segment.
  • New flavours and sustainable packaging can attract younger consumers who seek both convenience and responsibility.

Recommendations & Output

  • Product: Relaunch classic Nitro Cold Brew; introduce two seasonal flavours; design sleek matte cans with recyclable aluminium and resealable lids; add QR codes that tell the Nitro story.
  • Packaging: Emphasize sustainability with 100 % recyclable materials and minimal waste.
  • Distribution: Expand availability in grocery chains, cafés and online stores across Ontario, Quebec and BC.
  • Promotion: Use story‑driven social media, influencer collaborations and limited‑edition bundles to create buzz.
  • Timeline: Q1 – finalise packaging; Q2 – start production and develop flavours; Q3 – launch in Ontario and Quebec; Q4 – expand to BC/Alberta and release a holiday flavour.

Reflection

This plan reinforced the value of aligning brand identity with market trends. I learned to set measurable objectives, balance creativity with feasibility and coordinate marketing activities across a year‑long timeline. Next time, I’d incorporate consumer surveys and taste tests to fine‑tune flavour development and pricing.
