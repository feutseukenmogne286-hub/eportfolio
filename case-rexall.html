Rexall Valentine Merchandising

Auditing a store to bring candles into the Valentine shopping flow.

Quick Facts

  • Course: Not provided in the report
  • Type: Individual project (field observation and analysis)
  • Deliverables: Store‑check report and merchandising plan
  • Tools: Field observation; photos of store displays (Figures 2–6)

Objective

Identify merchandising opportunities to attract Valentine shoppers looking for candles using observations of existing displays and promotions.

My Role

  • Conducted an in‑store audit of product locations, end‑caps, secondary displays and point‑of‑sale promotions.
  • Documented pricing, promotional messages and expiration dates.
  • Noted the placement of candles and recorded that they were only on a back‑wall facing.
  • Developed a merchandising recommendation to move candles to a front‑of‑store display.

Approach & Frameworks

  • Walked the store and captured photos of end‑caps (e.l.f. beauty), secondary displays (Boost beverages, nail products) and Valentine confection POS.
  • Analysed how shoppers flow through the store and where impulse purchases occur.
  • Compared candle placement with the prominent candy display at checkout.
  • Designed a four‑sided floor stand concept to reposition candles near Valentine traffic.

Key Insights

  • Candles were easy to miss; they were on a back wall near groceries, not in the main traffic flow.
  • Rexall uses temporary seasonal displays effectively for candy, with clear dates and price drops.
  • Emotional gifts like candles should leverage the same impulse‑buy mechanics as confectionery.
  • A four‑sided stand near the front action alley would catch shoppers’ attention and tie into Valentine promotions.

Recommendations & Output

  • Introduce a 4‑sided floor stand (weighted base, 16 shelves) located in the main path to checkout, 3–6 metres before the Valentine confection display.
  • Stock 48–70 facings: entry‑price singles ($8.99–$11.99), mid‑range candles ($12.99–$15.99), high‑value jars (~$21.99) and gift sets.
  • Place gift sets at eye level, popular scents in the middle and entry items at the bottom.
  • Match Rexall’s existing promotional style: multi‑buy offers (“2 for $X”) and feature pricing.
  • Set up the display 14–24 days before Valentine’s Day and convert it to clearance after the holiday.

Reflection

This store audit sharpened my ability to observe retail environments and translate them into actionable merchandising plans. Seeing how a simple location change can make or break a product’s visibility reminded me that shopper flow and display design are critical. I would next test the proposed display, measure sales impact and refine the product mix based on results.
